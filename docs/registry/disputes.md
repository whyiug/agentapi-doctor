# Disputes, Supersede, and Withdrawal

A public observation is contestable. A future Registry must preserve both the
immutable submitted fact and a visible, auditable lifecycle around it.

## Lifecycle

1. A claimant identifies the exact observation digest and authority/interest.
2. The Registry records a bounded dispute without changing the observation.
3. The subject owner may attach a signed response.
4. New evidence may supersede an old observation by digest.
5. Invalid, unsafe, or withdrawn material may be tombstoned from public views.
6. Physical artifacts follow the applicable deletion/backup schedule while
   minimal audit facts remain as law and policy allow.

Every action records actor authority, reason class, time, subject digest, and
audit event. A vendor cannot decide its own dispute alone, and payment or
sponsorship cannot remove a valid failure. Appeals require an unconflicted
reviewer.

This process has not been operated or drilled against a hosted service.
