# Maintainer Meeting Process

No maintainer meeting cadence has started. If the project has enough active
participants to sustain one, it may schedule a public monthly meeting with:

- an agenda published before the meeting;
- remote access and time-zone information;
- a named facilitator and note taker;
- links to RFCs/issues rather than decisions made only in conversation;
- an opportunity for asynchronous input; and
- concise minutes published after participant review.

Minutes record attendees who consent to be named, topics, factual decisions,
action owner/target, conflicts/recusals, and links to the authoritative RFC or
artifact. Attendance is not a governance vote unless the current governance
policy explicitly defines that vote.

Security embargoes, conduct reports, private data, credentials, and personal
matters are excluded from public minutes. A non-sensitive note may state that a
private topic was deferred without describing it.

Use [the meeting template](TEMPLATE.md) only after a meeting actually occurs.
Do not pre-fill future attendance, decisions, or endorsements.
