# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in the AgentAPI Doctor community a
harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size,
visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender
identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic
status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or
sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people;
- respecting differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences;
- giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback;
- accepting responsibility, apologizing to those affected by mistakes, and
  learning from the experience; and
- focusing on what is best for the community and the project's users.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- sexualized language or imagery, sexual attention, or advances;
- trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political
  attacks;
- public or private harassment;
- publishing another person's private information without explicit
  permission;
- pressuring someone to disclose an employer, affiliation, identity, or
  conflict beyond what a particular governance decision requires; and
- conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional
  setting.

## Enforcement responsibilities

The current maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these
standards and may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and
other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct. Enforcement
must be proportionate, documented where privacy permits, and free of conflicts
of interest.

A maintainer who is the subject of a report must not investigate or decide
that report. If no unconflicted project maintainer is available, the matter
remains unresolved at project level until an independent reviewer can be
appointed; platform-level abuse may be reported directly to GitHub.

## Scope

This Code applies in project spaces and when an individual officially
represents the project in public spaces. It does not create authority over
unrelated personal activity.

## Reporting

Do not place sensitive conduct details in a public issue. Contact the lead
maintainer through the contact method published on
[@whyiug's GitHub profile](https://github.com/whyiug) when the report does not
involve that maintainer. The private vulnerability form is reserved for
security vulnerabilities and must not be used as a general conduct inbox.

GitHub-hosted abuse, or a report involving the sole maintainer, should use
[GitHub's abuse reporting process](https://support.github.com/contact/report-abuse).
The project does not currently claim an independent confidential conduct panel.

The project does not promise a response deadline. A report should include the
relevant links or dates, the behavior observed, its impact, and any immediate
safety concern. Only information needed to investigate should be shared.

## Enforcement guidelines

Maintainers should apply the following community-impact guidelines while
considering context, severity, recurrence, safety, and conflicts of interest:

1. **Correction** — a private explanation and request to correct minor,
   isolated inappropriate behavior.
2. **Warning** — a clear warning for a violation, with boundaries on further
   interaction and consequences for repetition.
3. **Temporary restriction** — a time-limited restriction from project
   interaction for sustained or serious behavior.
4. **Permanent restriction** — removal from project interaction for a pattern
   of violations, harassment, threats, or other severe misconduct.

Appeals must be reviewed by an unconflicted maintainer or independent reviewer.
The project will not publish a reporter's identity or unnecessary private
details.
